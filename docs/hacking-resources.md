# Hacking Resources

Hack The Break 2026 is all about building and creating, and we want to make sure you have all the resources you need to succeed! Below is a curated list of resources that will be available to you during the event, including mentor details, workshop information, and more.

---

## Mentors

Mentors are experienced professionals and students who will be available throughout the event to provide guidance, answer questions, and help you troubleshoot any issues you may encounter.

### Contacting Mentors
You can reach out to mentors in the following ways:

- **In-Person**: Mentors will be circulating the event space during the hacking period. Look for them wearing mentor badges and don't hesitate to approach them with your questions or ideas.
- **Discord**: We will have a dedicated mentor channel on our Discord server where you can ask questions and get help remotely. Mentors will be monitoring this channel during the event, so feel free to post your questions there and give them a ping using `@Mentor` to get their attention.
- **Mentor Sessions**: We will be hosting scheduled mentor sessions throughout the event where you can sign up for one-on-one time with a mentor to get personalized help with your project. Keep an eye on the schedule and announcements for when these sessions will be available.

### Mentor Schedule
We will have mentors available during the following times:

- **Friday**: 7:00 PM - 9:00 PM
- **Saturday**: 10:00 AM - 10:00 PM
- **Sunday**: 10:00 AM - 11:00 AM

!!! question "Having trouble outside of mentor hours?"
    If you need help outside of the scheduled mentor hours, you can still ask for help in the Discord mentor channel. The organizers will be present at all times and also will be mentoring participants in their free time.

### Mentor FAQ

!!! question "What kind of help can mentors provide?"
    Mentors can help with a wide range of topics, including:
    - Brainstorming and ideation
    - Technical questions and troubleshooting
    - Guidance on project scope and features
    - Feedback on project ideas and presentations
    - Deployment and hosting advice

!!! question "Do I need to have a fully formed idea before approaching a mentor?"
    Not at all! Mentors are there to help you at any stage of the project, whether you're just starting to brainstorm ideas or you're deep into development and need help with a specific issue. Don't hesitate to reach out to a mentor even if you just have a rough idea or need help getting started.

## Workshops
In addition to mentor support, we will also be hosting workshops on various topics to help you learn new skills and technologies that you can use in your projects. These workshops will be led by industry professionals, experienced students, and event organizers.

Below are some of the workshops we have planned for HackTheBreak 2026:

Join us for an evening bootcamp on the full hackathon process, from creating a team to your final demo. nwPlus will be sharing tips and tricks to help ensure your first hackathon project gets you up on the main stage. 

    - Location: SW03 - 1750
    - Date & Time: Tuesday, March 3rd @5:30-7:00PM

!!! example "BCIT C4A x BCIT Computing Club - Introduction to Deploying Workshop"

Whether you're preparing for a hackathon or building your portfolio, you'll learn how to take your project from local development to a live, shareable product ready for judges, friends, LinkedIn, and beyond. 

    - Location: SW01 - 1021
    - Date & Time: Tuesday, March 10th @5:30-7:00PM

!!! example "BCIT WiC X BCIT Computing Club - Introduction to Pitching and Project Ideation Workshop"

Join us for an interactive workshop on developing strong, creative project concepts and confidently presenting them in a way that leaves a lasting impression. Whether you're preparing for a hackathon, a class project, or a startup idea, you'll learn practical strategies to sharpen your ideas, structure a compelling pitch, and stand out to judges and audiences alike.
