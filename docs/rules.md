# Rules, Guidelines, and FAQ

## Rules
We're excited to have you here! To ensure this event remains a safe, inclusive, and productive space for everyone, please follow the rules below:

### Event Rules
1. Eligibility
    - Participants must be current BCIT students or alumni.
    - Teams can consist of up to 4 members (up to 5 if at least one member is a first-semester student).

2. Code of Conduct
    - Follow the BCIT Code of Conduct at all times: [BCIT Code of Conduct](https://www.bcit.ca/about/administration/policies/code-of-conduct/).
    - Be respectful to all participants, mentors, volunteers, and organizers.
    - No harassment, discrimination, or offensive behavior will be tolerated.

3. Safety
    - Follow all safety guidelines provided by event staff.
    - Report any safety concerns or incidents to event organizers immediately.

4. Follow Organizer Instructions
    - Please listen to and follow instructions from event staff, mentors, and volunteers.
    - Failure to comply with staff instructions may result in removal from the event.

---

### Project Rules

1. Project Guidelines
    - Projects must be built during the event timeframe (March 13-15, 2026).
    - Projects must be submitted on Devpost by the Sunday 11:00 AM deadline.
    - Projects should not violate any laws, terms of service, or BCIT policies.
    - Plagiarism or academic misconduct will result in disqualification.
    - Projects must be appropriate for all audiences (no NSFW or offensive content).

2. AI Usage
    - AI tools can be used for brainstorming, prototyping, and development.
    - Participants are responsible for ensuring that any AI-generated content complies with all event rules and guidelines.
    - AI-generated code must be reviewed and understood by the participant using it.

---

### Discord Rules

1. Follow Discord's Community Guidelines and Terms of Service
    - All standard Discord rules apply at all times.

2. Respect Staff and Organizers
    - Please listen to and follow instructions from moderators, mentors, and event staff.

3. English Only
    - This is an English-speaking server. Please communicate in English to the best of your ability.

4. No Illegal, Unethical, or Academic Misconduct Content
    - Do not request or provide help with:
        - Illegal activities
        - Breaching terms of service
        - Violating BCIT's Academic Code of Conduct
        - Malicious or inappropriate projects
        - Graded coursework or exams

5. No Spam or Unauthorized Promotion
    - This includes excessive messages, self-promotion, advertisements, or requests for paid work without approval.

6. Stay On Topic
    - Please keep conversations relevant to the purpose and topic of each channel.

7. Be Respectful
    - No slurs, harassment, hate speech, or offensive language of any kind.

8. No NSFW or Inappropriate Content
    - This includes explicit, suggestive, or otherwise inappropriate material.

---

## FAQ

!!! question "Can I participate if I'm not a BCIT student?"
    No, HackTheBreak is exclusive to BCIT students and alumni. We want to create a space for our community to come together and collaborate.
!!! question "Can I form a team with people from different programs or campuses?"
    Yes! You can form teams with participants from any program or campus as long as they are BCIT students or alumni.
!!! question "Can I participate solo?"
    Yes, you can participate solo if you prefer. Many participants choose to work alone, and that's perfectly fine!
!!! question "What if I don't have a team?"
    No worries! Many participants form teams at the event. You can attend collaborative workshops, use the team formation channel in Discord, or join the team formation session during the opening ceremony to meet other participants looking for teammates.
!!! question "Can I use AI tools to help with my project?"
    Yes, you can use AI tools for brainstorming, prototyping, and development. However, you are responsible for ensuring that any AI-generated content complies with all event rules and guidelines. Make sure to review and understand any AI-generated code or content you use in your project.
!!! question "What if I have a question or need help during the event?"
    You can ask for help in the appropriate channels on Discord, attend mentor sessions, or approach event staff in person. We're here to support you!
